Haunts

The Ridges

Athens, Ohio

Located in the beautiful Athens, The Ridges or The Athens Lunatic Asylum operated from 1874 to 1993. It is the eeriest of the haunted spots for many reasons. The batwing layout as well as it's grand entrance and gothic style adds to the creepiness. 1,930 people are buried among the three cemeteries that occupy the grounds of the asylum. One of the most famous hauntings of the building is that of Margaret Schilling. She was a patient at the asylum who went missing only to be found a month later decomposing on the floor of an unused room. The stain on the floor and her ghost remain today.

Moonville Tunnel

McArthur, Ohio

Constructed in 1856 and located deep in the Zaleski State Forest, the tunnel used to be a train tunnel that serviced a small mining community. By the 1900s the town became obsolete and the tunnel no longer became used. During its time in operation, however, there was a lot of activity through the tunnel and there are rumored to be many hauntings from people who died on the train tracks.

King's Hallow Tunnel

New Marshfield, Ohio

Also Located in Zaleski State Forest, this tunnel was built in the 1850s out of large timber beams through the side of a cliff, this tunnel is very similar to Moonville Tunnel. On one end of the tunnel, you can still find the foundations from the town that used to thrive in this area. It is rumored that many ghosts still haunt the tunnel and abandoned town.

Silver Memorial Bridge

Gallipolis, Ohio - Point Pleasent, West Virginia

Spanning the Ohio River this bridge is the site of a deadly disaster. On December 15, 1967, the bridge collapsed during heavy traffic resulting in the deaths of 46 people. Although this tragedy is not the only reason it's famous. For a year leading up to the disaster, there were numerous sitings of the Mothman. Many believe it was warning people of the disaster to come. Today the bridge is rebuilt and in Point Pleasent, there is a Mothman statue and Museum for people to visit.

Old Mans Cave

Logan, Ohio

Located in the heart of Hocking Hills, Old Mans Cave is a ravine cut through the sandstone from erosion over thousands of years. Hiking trails encompass the whole area letting visitors explore the cave in its entirety. Numerous deaths and accidents have occurred over the years due to the high cave walls and paths that take visitors right to the edge. Many ghostly sightings have been reported.
